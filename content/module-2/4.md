## The three tasks

AI is particularly strong at three research-related operations:

1. **Research** — rapid survey of a topic, identifying key concepts, terminology, competing positions
2. **Analysis** — applying a framework to a document, argument, or dataset to surface structure
3. **Synthesis** — combining multiple sources or ideas into a coherent summary or argument

These are also the tasks that consume most of a knowledge worker's time.

## Research: using AI as a scout

Instead of spending two hours surveying a field, spend 20 minutes asking questions:

- "What are the three main schools of thought on X?"
- "What does the current literature say about Y?"
- "What are the most common objections to Z?"

**Critical rule:** AI-generated research is a starting point, not a conclusion. Verify key claims. The AI may cite real papers incorrectly, invent plausible-sounding references, or miss recent developments. Use it to build your map, then verify the landmarks.

## Analysis: applying frameworks

Give the AI a document and a framework. Ask it to apply one to the other.

Examples:
- "Analyse this contract for potential obligations and risks. Highlight anything unusual."
- "Apply a SWOT analysis to this business proposal."
- "Read this argument. What are its premises and conclusions? Where is the logic weakest?"

The AI is not making final judgments — it's doing the first pass that would otherwise take you an hour.

## Synthesis: combining sources

When you have multiple inputs — research notes, meeting transcripts, a set of articles — AI can synthesise them faster than you can read them.

Paste the sources and ask: "These are my notes from five different reports on X. Write a 500-word synthesis identifying the key conclusions and where the sources disagree."

## The rule that makes all of this work

**You are the expert. The AI is a fast, tireless research assistant.** It covers ground quickly. You spot what's wrong. The combination is powerful; either alone is limited.

## Key takeaway

AI accelerates research, analysis, and synthesis — but the judgment layer is yours. Build the habit of using AI for the first draft of thinking, then apply your own expertise to refine it.
