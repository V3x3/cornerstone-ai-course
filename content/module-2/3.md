## What you're doing and why

Reading about prompting is useful. Doing it on a real task is how the skill actually lands. This exercise takes something from your actual work and improves it using AI — in under 10 minutes.

## Choose your document

Pick one of:
- An email you're about to send that isn't quite right yet
- A section of a report or proposal you've been putting off
- A job posting, announcement, or communications piece that needs tightening
- A meeting summary from this week

It should be something real. The exercise works better when you care about the output.

## The process

**Step 1: Paste the draft into your AI of choice with this prompt:**
> "I'm going to paste a draft document. Read it carefully. Then rewrite it to be clearer, more direct, and better structured. Keep the same meaning. Do not add content I didn't include."

**Step 2: Review the output.** Compare it to your original. What did the AI change? Is the meaning preserved?

**Step 3: Push back on anything you don't like.** Tell the AI specifically what to fix: "The tone is too formal — make it warmer." Or "The second paragraph is better in my version — use that instead."

**Step 4: Extract the best version.** You are the editor. The AI is drafting. You decide what's good.

## What to notice

- Did the AI improve the structure or just rearrange words?
- Did it preserve your voice, or flatten it?
- Did you learn anything about your own writing from the comparison?

## The key skill

The AI is not replacing your judgment — it's giving you a fast second draft to react to. That reaction is where your thinking happens. The best use of AI in writing is as a mirror that makes your own ideas clearer.

## Key takeaway

You've now used AI for real work. The practice is: draft → AI rewrite → you edit → done. This loop can save hours per week if you build it into your workflow.
