## The exercise

This module covered the outsourcing trap, the centaur model, epistemic humility, asymmetric AI use, and the long game. The dependency audit is the capstone exercise for the course.

It has two parts: an honest accounting of where you are today, and a specific design for where you want to be.

---

## Part 1: The dependency audit (15 minutes)

List the ten most significant ways you use AI in your professional life. For each, work through the following:

**For each AI use:**

**Leverage assessment:**
- Does this use give me capability I would not otherwise have (high leverage), or does it make me faster at something I could already do adequately (low leverage)?
- Has this use taught me anything I did not know before, or does it produce outputs I accept without deeply engaging with?

**Dependency assessment:**
- If my AI access were revoked tomorrow, could I perform this task at the quality my role requires?
- Is my ability to do this independently stronger, the same, or weaker than it was twelve months ago?

**Accountability assessment:**
- If asked, could I explain specifically why I accepted this AI output — not "it looked right" but a precise account of the judgment I applied?
- Has this AI use reduced my exposure to making consequential errors, or has it introduced a new kind of error (accepting AI output that is subtly wrong) that I would not have made before?

---

## Part 2: The map (5–10 minutes)

Place each of your ten AI uses in one of four categories:

**High leverage, low dependency:** You gain genuine capability. Your independent ability is maintained or growing. This is the centaur model working correctly.

**High leverage, high dependency:** The use is genuinely powerful, but you have delegated judgment along with execution. You need to either reclaim the judgment or explicitly decide the dependency is acceptable given the stakes.

**Low leverage, low dependency:** Convenient but not compounding. Acceptable for genuinely low-stakes tasks.

**Low leverage, high dependency:** The most urgent category. You are dependent on AI for tasks where the leverage is minimal and the stakes are real. Address these first.

---

## Part 3: The 90-day commitment (10 minutes)

Based on your audit, make three specific commitments.

**Commitment 1: Something to reclaim.** Identify one task where you have drifted from centaur to passenger — where you are accepting AI output without adequate judgment. Describe exactly how you will apply genuine judgment to this task going forward.

**Commitment 2: Something to redirect.** Identify one low-leverage AI use that is consuming time you could redirect toward high-leverage use. Describe what you will replace it with and how.

**Commitment 3: The weekly review.** Design your version of the Friday review described in the final lesson. Specifically:
- When will you do it? (Day, time — be precise.)
- What three questions will you ask yourself?
- How will you use AI to support the reflection without delegating it?

Write the commitment in the first person, in present tense, as a description of what you already do: "Every Friday at 5pm, I review the week's decisions by asking myself..."

Writing it this way is not a trick. It is how habits form: as descriptions of identity, not aspirations.

---

## The course commitment

You have completed five modules covering how AI works, how your mind works, how professional games are structured, how careers compound, and how to delegate without becoming dependent.

The frameworks are now yours. What happens next is not a function of the course — it is a function of whether you build the habits that activate them.

The dependency audit is not a one-time exercise. Run it quarterly. The map will shift as your AI use evolves. New dependencies will form. New high-leverage uses will emerge. The discipline is maintaining the visibility to see the map as it actually is, not as you assume it to be.

The only thing this course cannot do is make you use it.

That part is yours.
