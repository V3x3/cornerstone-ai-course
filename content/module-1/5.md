## What you're doing and why

You've learned how LLMs work — prediction, not truth. Hallucinations. Limits. Now you'll experience them directly.

This exercise has two goals:
1. Build a concrete sense of where AI fails
2. Start developing your own calibration — knowing when to trust it and when to verify

## The exercise

Choose any AI tool (Claude, ChatGPT, or Gemini — free tiers are fine).

Run each of the following tests and write down what you observe:

### Test 1: Specific facts
Ask the AI a specific factual question about something you know well. A person in your field, a local event, a technical detail from your domain. Verify the answer independently. Was it right? Confident even when wrong?

### Test 2: Recent events
Ask about something that happened in the last 6 months. Observe how the model handles its knowledge cutoff — does it acknowledge it or proceed confidently?

### Test 3: Citations
Ask the model to provide 3 references on a topic you know. Try to find those references. How many actually exist?

### Test 4: Push on the wrong answer
If the model gives you an answer, push back — even if it was right. Say "Are you sure? I heard it was X." How does it respond? Does it capitulate or hold the correct position?

## What to record

Write a short note (even bullet points) covering:
- What broke, and how it broke
- What surprised you — positively or negatively
- One thing you'll now verify that you might not have before

## Why this matters

You are building a mental model of this tool's failure modes. That model will make you a more effective and safer user of AI than someone who has only seen it succeed.

## Key takeaway

Trust comes from understanding limits. After this exercise, you'll use AI with more confidence — because you'll know exactly when not to trust it.
